This guide summarizes the excellent 5-day itinerary documented by [Travelsmith](https://travelsmith.travelmap.net/mae-hong-son-loop-chiang-mai-thailand). It's a fantastic route for those who want to see all the nature highlights, including the highest peak in Thailand.

---

## 🗺️ Route Overview
*   **Duration:** 5 Days
*   **Total Distance:** ~750 km (466 miles)
*   **Direction:** Counter-Clockwise (Chiang Mai → Pai → Mae Hong Son → Mae Sariang → Chiang Mai)
*   **The Main Achievement:** 1,864 curves (don't forget to grab your certificate in MHS City!)

---

## 🗓️ Day-by-Day Highlights

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Pai
*   **Mok Fa Waterfall:** A lush detour at Km 20 on the way to Pai. Note: Requires a short 2km dirt road drive.
*   **Pong Dueat Geyser:** Thailand's largest geyser (Huai Nam Dang NP), with water reaching 150°C.
*   **Pai Entrance:** Stop at the Historical WWII Memorial Bridge and Pai Canyon for sunset.

### Day 2: Around Pai
*   **Culture:** Visit Santichon Village (Yunnanese tradition) and Wat Nam Hu.
*   **Views:** Sunrise at the Yun Lai viewpoint and sunset at the White Big Buddha (Wat Phra That Mae Yen).
*   **Evening:** Pai Walking Street for local food and markets.

### Day 3: Pai to Mae Hong Son
*   **Caves & Nature:** Explore Lod Cave (Tham Lod) and the Fish Cave (Tham Pla).
*   **Lakeside Serenity:** Ban Rak Thai, a picturesque Yunnanese village near the Myanmar border.
*   **City Views:** Sunset at Wat Phra That Doi Kong Mu for a panorama of Mae Hong Son City.

### Day 4: Mae Hong Son to Mae Sariang
*   **Mae Surin Waterfall:** One of the spectacular, high-drop waterfalls in the region.
*   **Historical Pause:** Visit the Thai-Japan WWII Museum in Khun Yuam.
*   **Sleepy Town:** Overnight in the quiet river town of Mae Sariang.

### Day 5: Mae Sariang to Chiang Mai (via Doi Inthanon)
*   **The Roof of Thailand:** Ascend Doi Inthanon National Park, the highest point in the country.
*   **Twin Pagodas:** The Great Holy Relics pagodas and the surrounding royal gardens.
*   **Waterfall Finale:** Stop at Wachiratan and the massive Mae Ya waterfall before returning to Chiang Mai.

---

## 💡 Travelsmith Tips
- **Gravel & Dust:** Be cautious on the turn-offs (like Mok Fa), especially in the dry season.
- **Geyser Timing:** Pong Dueat springs up every 30 seconds; keep your distance as the water is boiling (150°C).
- **Certificate:** You can officially claim your "1,864 Curves Survivor" certificate at the chamber of commerce in Mae Hong Son city.
