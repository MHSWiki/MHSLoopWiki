Heading **Clockwise** (Chiang Mai -> Mae Sariang -> Mae Hong Son -> Pai) is the most popular choice for first-time loop riders. 

**Why choose Clockwise?**
Highway 108 (South) is a wide, well-paved dual-lane road for much of the first 100km. It allows you to get comfortable with your bike and the Thai road system before you hit the steep, technical switchbacks on the road to Mae Hong Son and Pai.

    - **Night 1:** [Mae Sariang](/towns/mae-sariang) (Riverhouse or Above the Sea).
    - **Night 2:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) (Gode View or Bunk Boutique).

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="4-Day (The Standard)">

    This is the "Sweet Spot" itinerary. It allows for a relaxed pace and time to explore the major viewpoints without feeling rushed.

    | Day | Route | Distance | Focus |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | **1** | Chiang Mai ➔ Mae Sariang | 190 km | Relaxed ride, afternoon river cruise in MS. |
    | **2** | Mae Sariang ➔ MHS City | 165 km | Stop at Khun Yuam and the Mexican Sunflower fields. |
    | **3** | MHS City ➔ Pai | 110 km | Narrow, steep passes. Lunch at Soppong. |
    | **4** | Pai ➔ Chiang Mai | 135 km | Coffee stops and the famous descent to the 107. |

    **What do you get for the 4th day?** 
    You get to stay overnight in **Pai**. This means you can enjoy the famous Pai Walking Street and live music scene without worrying about a 4-hour ride back to Chiang Mai the same evening.

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="5-Day (The Deep Dive)">

    The 5-day loop is for those who want to see everything. It includes the "Long Neck" villages and the lakeside beauty of Ban Rak Thai.

    | Day | Route | Distance | Focus |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

    | **3** | MHS City ➔ Ban Rak Thai | 45 km | Steep technical climb to the Chinese Tea Village. |

    **What do you get for the 5th day?**
    An overnight stay in **Ban Rak Thai**. Waking up to the morning mist over the lake while drinking locally grown Oolong tea is considered by many to be the highlight of the entire trip.

## 🗺️ "Have More Time?" — Side Trips & Detours

If you have 6+ days or want to add some extra grit to your loop, consider these additions:

### 1. The Doi Inthanon Summit (Day Trip or Overnight)
*   **From:** Chiang Mai or Mae Chaem.
*   **Action:** Ride to the highest point in Thailand (2,565m). 
*   **Where to Stay:** Stay in **Mae Chaem** for a very local, non-touristy experience before rejoining the loop at Khun Yuam.

### 2. The Salween River Border (Half-Day Detour)
*   **From:** Mae Sariang.
*   **Action:** Ride Highway 1194 to the end. Hire a long-tail boat to cruise the Salween River (the border with Myanmar).
*   **Where to Stay:** Return to your base in **Mae Sariang**.

### 3. Tham Lod Cave Expedition
*   **From:** Between MHS and Pai (Soppong).
*   **Action:** Spend a full 4 hours exploring the massive cave system with a local guide and lantern.
*   **Where to Stay:** Spend a night in **Soppong** (Cave Lodge) for an authentic jungle experience.

### 4. Wat Chan & The Pine Forests
*   **From:** Pai.
*   **Action:** Instead of the main 1095, take the "back road" (Highway 1265) through the largest pine forests in SE Asia.
*   **Where to Stay:** Start in Pai, end in **Samoeng** or return to Chiang Mai.

<Aside type="caution" title="Plan your fuel!">
Gas stations become significantly less frequent between Mae Sariang and Mae Hong Son. Always top up in the major towns before heading into the mountain sections.
</Aside>
