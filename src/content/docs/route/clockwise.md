**Heading Clockwise** (Chiang Mai ➔ Mae Sariang ➔ Mae Hong Son ➔ Pai) is the most popular choice for first-time loop riders. 

**Why choose Clockwise?**
Highway 108 (South) from Chiang Mai to Mae Sariang is a wide, well-paved dual-lane road for much of the first 100km. It allows you to get comfortable with your rented bike and the Thai road system before you hit the steep, technical switchbacks on the road from Mae Sariang to Mae Hong Son and Pai.

  <TabItem label="3-Day (The Express)">
    
The 3-day loop is a true test of endurance. You will spend 5–7 hours in the saddle each day minimum. Highly recommended only for experienced riders who are short on time.

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Mae Sariang
**Distance:** 190 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4.5 hours
**The Ride:** Your journey begins on Highway 108 South. The first 50 km are mostly flat highway to get out of the Chiang Mai traffic. The real fun begins after Chom Thong as the road starts to gently sweep through the pine forests of Ob Luang National Park.
*   **KM 85 - Ob Luang National Park:** A perfect mid-morning break. Park the bike and take a 20-minute walk to see the narrow river gorge.
*   **KM 130 - Suan Son Bor Kaew (Pine Tree Garden):** Stunning rows of towering pine trees, an ideal spot for a quick photo and a stretch.
*   **KM 190 - Mae Sariang:** Arrive in time for sunset over the Yuam River.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Sariang](/towns/mae-sariang) — **Riverhouse Resort** (for premium comfort) or **The Riverbank** (for undercover parking).

### Day 2: Mae Sariang to Mae Hong Son City
**Distance:** 165 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4 hours
**The Ride:** The road gets narrower and the forest gets thicker. Highway 108 continues north, featuring enjoyable, mid-speed winding roads.
*   **KM 90 - Khun Yuam:** Stop here for lunch and fuel. Visit the Thai-Japan Friendship Memorial Hall if you enjoy WWII history. 
*   **KM 115 - Pha Bong Viewpoint:** A highly recommended pull-off with a small cafe overlooking a gorgeous valley.
*   **KM 165 - Mae Hong Son City:** Check into your hotel, then head straight to Nong Chong Kham Lake for the night market.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) — **Piya Guesthouse** (right by the lake) or **Bunk Boutique**. 

### Day 3: Mae Hong Son to Chiang Mai (via Pai)
**Distance:** 245 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 6-7 hours
**The Ride:** This is a monster day. **Start early (by 8:00 AM).** You will tackle the famous 762 curves back-to-back, passing through Pai and then descending into the Chiang Mai valley.
*   **KM 65 - Jabo Village Viewpoint:** A breathtaking spot for morning coffee, suspended over the valley. 
*   **KM 110 - Pai:** Grab a solid lunch at Om Garden Cafe and refuel. Do not linger too long; you still have 135 km of intense mountain riding left.
*   **KM 245 - Chiang Mai:** Return your bike before the shops close at 6:00 PM. Follow Highway 107 back into the city carefully, as traffic will be heavy.

  <TabItem label="4-Day (The Standard)">

This is the "Sweet Spot" itinerary. It allows for a relaxed pace with enough time for decent lunches, viewpoints, and taking it all in without feeling exhausted.

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Mae Sariang
**Distance:** 190 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4.5 hours
**The Ride:** Highway 108 South provides a forgiving introduction to the trip. 
*   **KM 85 - Ob Luang National Park:** Stretch your legs and see the river gorge.
*   **KM 130 - Pine Tree Garden (Suan Son Bor Kaew):** Great photo op in the towering pines.
*   **KM 190 - Mae Sariang:** Settle in, grab a beer at Sawadee Bar, and watch the sun go down.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Sariang](/towns/mae-sariang) — **Riverhouse Resort** or **Above The Sea**.

### Day 2: Mae Sariang to Mae Hong Son City
**Distance:** 165 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4 hours
**The Ride:** The curves tighten slightly as you progress north. 
*   **KM 90 - Khun Yuam:** Fuel stop and lunch. If traveling in November/December, detour to the Thung Bua Tong (Mexican Sunflower) fields.
*   **KM 115 - Pha Bong Viewpoint:** Grab an iced coffee and enjoy the panoramic canyon view.
*   **KM 165 - Mae Hong Son City:** Spend the evening eating street food around Nong Chong Kham Lake and visit Wat Chong Kham illuminated at night.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) — **Piya Guesthouse** or **Fern Resort** (for a jungle luxury feel just outside town).

### Day 3: Mae Hong Son City to Pai
**Distance:** 110 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3 hours
**The Ride:** The most scenic and dramatic section of the loop. Constant elevation changes and sharp switchbacks on Highway 1095.
*   **KM 65 - Jabo Village (Noodle Soup House):** The ultimate breakfast/coffee stop with legs dangling over the cliff edge.
*   **KM 75 - Tham Lod Cave:** (Optional detour) Add 2 hours to explore this massive river cave on a bamboo raft.
*   **KM 110 - Pai:** Arrive by early afternoon. Enjoy sunset at Pai Canyon, then head to Walking Street for food and live music at Jazz House.

**Stay Strategy:** [Pai](/towns/pai) — **The Reverie** (luxury) or **Family House Zen Boutique**.

### Day 4: Pai to Chiang Mai
**Distance:** 135 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3.5 hours
**The Ride:** The infamous "762 curves" section. It's steep, twisty, and busy with minivans. Take it slow and stay in your lane.
*   **KM 30 - Memorial Bridge:** A good spot to stretch just as you leave Pai. 
*   **KM 90 - 32 Coffee Hill:** Excellent halfway pit stop for a final hit of caffeine and a pastry before the mountains end.
*   **KM 135 - Chiang Mai:** Highway 1095 merges into the fast-paced Highway 107. Be prepared for aggressive city traffic for the last 40 km.

  <TabItem label="5-Day (The Deep Dive)">

The 5-day loop is for those who want to see everything and truly immerse themselves in the Northern Thai mountains.

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Mae Sariang
**Distance:** 190 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4.5 hours
**The Ride:** Head south on Hwy 108. 
*   **KM 85 - Ob Luang:** Short hike at the river gorge.
*   **KM 190 - Mae Sariang:** Enjoy a very relaxed afternoon. Consider a long-tail boat ride on the nearby Salween River if you arrive early enough.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Sariang](/towns/mae-sariang) — **The Riverbank** or **Above The Sea**.

### Day 2: Mae Sariang to Mae Hong Son City
**Distance:** 165 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4 hours
**The Ride:** Cruising north through the valleys.
*   **KM 90 - Khun Yuam:** Stop for a great local lunch by the roadside restaurants.
*   **KM 165 - Mae Hong Son City:** Arrive mid-afternoon. Visit the hilltop **Wat Phra That Doi Kong Mu** for sunset over the city.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) — **Piya Guesthouse**.

### Day 3: Mae Hong Son City to Ban Rak Thai
**Distance:** 45 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 1.5 hours
**The Ride:** A short but incredibly steep and technical climb up to the Myanmar border. 
*   **KM 15 - Su Tong Pae Bridge:** A beautiful bamboo bridge built by locals spanning over rice paddies up to a temple. Best visited in the early morning.
*   **KM 45 - Ban Rak Thai:** Spend the entire afternoon exploring this stunning Chinese tea village built around a lake. Drink Oolong tea and eat Yunnanese pork knuckle.

**Stay Strategy:** **Ban Rak Thai** — **Lee Wine Ruk Thai Resort** (book well in advance!) or **Chasa Rakthai**.

### Day 4: Ban Rak Thai to Pai
**Distance:** 120 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3 hours
**The Ride:** Descend from the tea village and join the 1095 eastward.
*   **KM 30 - Pha Suea Waterfall:** A quick stop to see one of the nicer waterfalls in the province.
*   **KM 75 - Jabo Village:** Pull over for incredible views and a strong coffee.
*   **KM 120 - Pai:** Check in, relax at an aesthetic cafe like Monko, and hit the Walking Street at night.

**Stay Strategy:** [Pai](/towns/pai) — **Pai Village Boutique Resort**.

### Day 5: Pai to Chiang Mai
**Distance:** 135 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3.5 hours
**The Ride:** The final test of your cornering skills down the rest of the 1095.
*   **KM 90 - 32 Coffee Hill:** Your last mountain break before hitting the plains.
*   **KM 135 - Chiang Mai:** Navigate the Highway 107 traffic and return your bike with a massive sense of accomplishment.

## 🗺️ "Have More Time?" — Side Trips & Detours

If you have 6+ days or want to add some extra grit to your loop, consider these additions:

### 1. The Doi Inthanon Summit (Day Trip or Overnight)
*   **From:** Chiang Mai or Mae Chaem.
*   **Action:** Ride to the highest point in Thailand (2,565m). Bring warm clothes, as it gets freezing at the top.
*   **Where to Stay:** Stay in **Mae Chaem** for a very local, non-touristy experience before rejoining the loop at Khun Yuam.

### 2. Tham Lod Cave Expedition
*   **From:** Between MHS and Pai (Soppong).
*   **Action:** Spend a full 4 hours exploring the massive cave system with a local guide and lantern.
*   **Where to Stay:** Spend a night in **Soppong** (Cave Lodge) for an authentic jungle experience.

### 3. Wat Chan & The Pine Forests
*   **From:** Pai.
*   **Action:** Instead of the main 1095, take the "back road" (Highway 1265) through the largest pine forests in SE Asia. It's remote and stunning.
*   **Where to Stay:** Start in Pai, end in **Samoeng** or return to Chiang Mai.

<Aside type="caution" title="Plan your fuel!">
Gas stations become significantly less frequent between Mae Sariang and Mae Hong Son. Always top up in the major towns before heading into the mountain sections. In rural areas, look for small roadside hand-cranked gas barrels.
</Aside>
