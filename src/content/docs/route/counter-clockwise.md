**Heading Counter-Clockwise** (Chiang Mai ➔ Pai ➔ Mae Hong Son ➔ Mae Sariang) is the choice for those who want to jump straight into the technical essence of the loop.

**Why choose Counter-Clockwise?**
You tackle the most famous (and most mentally taxing) section of the road—the 762 curves from Chiang Mai to Pai—on Day 1 while your energy, excitement, and concentration levels are at their absolute peak. It also means you finish your multi-day adventure on the faster, smoother, and wider Highway 108, which is a massive relief when you're fatigued on the final day.

  <TabItem label="3-Day (The Blitz)">
    
A highly fast-paced "lap" of the province. You hit the main stops, but you will be in the saddle for most of the daylight hours. Not recommended for beginners. 

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Pai
**Distance:** 135 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3.5 hours
**The Ride:** Head out of Chiang Mai via the busy Highway 107 north, turning left onto Highway 1095. This is where the magic begins. 
*   **KM 45 - 32 Coffee Hill:** Essential stop right as the serious mountain curves begin. Great coffee and pastries.
*   **KM 105 - Memorial Bridge:** A quick photo op just before entering the Pai valley.
*   **KM 135 - Pai:** Drop your bags and head to Pai Walking Street for street food, and Don't Cry Bar if you're feeling energetic.

**Stay Strategy:** [Pai](/towns/pai) — **Family House Zen Boutique** or **The Reverie**.

### Day 2: Pai to Mae Hong Son City
**Distance:** 110 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3 hours
**The Ride:** The most scenic and heavily elevated part of the loop. 
*   **KM 35 - Tham Lod Cave:** You have time for a quick 1-hour bamboo raft tour inside the cave.
*   **KM 45 - Jabo Village Viewpoint:** Stop at the famous cliff-hanging noodle shop for lunch and a coffee with your legs dangling into the abyss.
*   **KM 110 - Mae Hong Son City:** Arrive in the late afternoon. Catch the sunset from Wat Phra That Doi Kong Mu, then head to the lake for the night market.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) — **Piya Guesthouse** (best location on the lake).

### Day 3: Mae Hong Son City to Chiang Mai (via Mae Sariang)
**Distance:** 350 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 8-9 hours
**The Ride:** Prepare for a massive day of riding. This will push your endurance. 
*   **KM 50 - Pha Bong Viewpoint:** A quick stop for early morning coffee.
*   **KM 165 - Mae Sariang:** Stop for a proper lunch. We highly recommend Inthira Restaurant for their Yellow Curry.
*   **KM 270 - Ob Luang:** A brief stretch before the final push.
*   **KM 350 - Chiang Mai:** Follow Highway 108 back. Traffic gets heavy near the city, so ride extremely defensively.

  <TabItem label="4-Day (The Explorer)">

The most highly recommended way to see the loop counter-clockwise. Breaking up the massive final leg by stopping in Mae Sariang makes the trip infinitely more enjoyable.

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Pai
**Distance:** 135 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3.5 hours
**The Ride:** Attack the 762 curves while you are fresh. 
*   **KM 45 - 32 Coffee Hill:** Caffeine and sugar before the serious switchbacks.
*   **KM 135 - Pai:** Arrive by early afternoon. Head to Pai Canyon for sunset (wear good walking shoes, not riding boots!). 

**Stay Strategy:** [Pai](/towns/pai) — **Pai Village Boutique Resort**.

### Day 2: Pai to Mae Hong Son City
**Distance:** 110 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3 hours
**The Ride:** Sharp, steep mountain passes with breathtaking viewpoints.
*   **KM 45 - Jabo Village:** Park the bike and grab some noodles over the cliff edge.
*   **KM 95 - Su Tong Pae Bridge:** Detour slightly to walk across this stunning bamboo bridge built over rice paddies.
*   **KM 110 - Mae Hong Son City:** Unwind at a lakeside bar (like 77 House) and enjoy a quiet, atmospheric evening under the glowing temple lights.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) — **Bai Fern Guest House** or **Fern Resort**.

### Day 3: Mae Hong Son City to Mae Sariang
**Distance:** 165 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4 hours
**The Ride:** The road widens slightly and becomes a joyous, flowing ride through the forest.
*   **KM 50 - Pha Bong Viewpoint:** A quick pull-off for a coffee overlooking the valley.
*   **KM 75 - Khun Yuam:** Stop for lunch and fuel. Visit the World War II museum if interested.
*   **KM 165 - Mae Sariang:** Check into a riverside hotel. Head to Sawadee Bar for a beer and watch the sunset over the Yuam River. The perfect place to "cool down."

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Sariang](/towns/mae-sariang) — **Riverhouse Resort** or **The Riverbank**. 

### Day 4: Mae Sariang to Chiang Mai
**Distance:** 190 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4.5 hours
**The Ride:** The homeward stretch. The 108 is generally wide, fast, and relaxing.
*   **KM 60 - Pine Tree Garden (Suan Son Bor Kaew):** A peaceful stop among the towering pines.
*   **KM 105 - Ob Luang National Park:** A scenic river gorge right beside the highway, perfect for lunch or a snack break.
*   **KM 190 - Chiang Mai:** Arrive back in the city limits. Be hyper-aware of impatient local drivers as you enter the urban sprawl.

  <TabItem label="5-Day (The Slow & Deep)">

Total immersion. This splits up the mountain phases and gives you an overnight stay in the magical Chinese tea village of Ban Rak Thai.

### Day 1: Chiang Mai to Pai
**Distance:** 135 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3.5 hours
**The Ride:** Focus on the curves and arriving safely. 
*   **KM 45 - 32 Coffee Hill:** Relax before the climb.
*   **KM 135 - Pai:** Spend the afternoon café hopping (Khaotha Coffee or Om Garden) before joining the evening Walking Street crowd.

**Stay Strategy:** [Pai](/towns/pai) — **Family House Zen Boutique**.

### Day 2: Pai to Ban Rak Thai
**Distance:** 120 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 3 hours
**The Ride:** Deep into the mountains, passing through the Soppong ridges.
*   **KM 45 - Jabo Village:** Unbeatable views and coffee.
*   **KM 120 - Ban Rak Thai:** The last 15 km are incredibly steep—use engine braking! Spend the afternoon drinking tea and walking around the lakeside village. It feels like stepping into a historical Chinese painting.

**Stay Strategy:** **Ban Rak Thai** — **Lee Wine Ruk Thai Resort**.

### Day 3: Ban Rak Thai to Mae Hong Son City
**Distance:** 45 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 1.5 hours
**The Ride:** A short, steep descent back to the main loop. 
*   **KM 15 - Pha Suea Waterfall:** A beautiful midway stop.
*   **KM 30 - Su Tong Pae Bridge:** Walk the bamboo bridge over the paddies.
*   **KM 45 - Mae Hong Son City:** A very relaxed day. Get a Thai massage and eat dinner overlooking Nong Chong Kham lake.

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Hong Son City](/towns/mae-hong-son) — **Piya Guesthouse**.

### Day 4: Mae Hong Son City to Mae Sariang
**Distance:** 165 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4 hours
**The Ride:** Flowing curves heading south. 
*   **KM 75 - Khun Yuam:** Lunch and fuel.
*   **KM 165 - Mae Sariang:** Check into your riverside room and enjoy the tranquility of the loop's quietest town. 

**Stay Strategy:** [Mae Sariang](/towns/mae-sariang) — **Riverhouse Resort**.

### Day 5: Mae Sariang to Chiang Mai
**Distance:** 190 km | **Estimated Ride Time:** 4.5 hours
**The Ride:** Wide sweeping curves and highway back to civilization.
*   **KM 105 - Ob Luang National Park:** Great spot to stretch.
*   **KM 190 - Chiang Mai:** Drop the bike off and celebrate.

## 🗺️ "Have More Time?" — Side Trips & Detours

Even on a counter-clockwise loop, these detours are highly recommended if your schedule allows:

### 1. The Salween River Border (Half-Day)
*   **From:** Mae Sariang.
*   **Action:** Ride Highway 1194 to the end. Hire a long-tail boat to cruise the Salween River (the border with Myanmar).
*   **Stay:** Return to your base in **Mae Sariang**.

### 2. The 1263 "Mountain Shortcut" to Mae Chaem
*   **From:** Khun Yuam.
*   **Action:** Instead of going south to Mae Sariang, head East on the 1263. It's a rough, remote mountain road that leads to **Mae Chaem** directly at the base of Doi Inthanon.
*   **Stay:** **Mae Chaem** for a completely authentic, non-tourist Thai farming town experience.

### 3. Tham Lod Cave Expedition
*   **From:** Between Pai and MHS City (Soppong).
*   **Action:** Don't just ride past. Visit the massive Tham Lod cave and take a lantern raft.
*   **Stay:** **Cave Lodge** in Soppong for an authentic, legendary backpacker stay.

---

<Aside type="danger" title="Day 1 Warning: Don't Rush to Pai">
Highway 1095 from Chiang Mai to Pai is narrow and has significant, aggressive minivan traffic. If you're riding counter-clockwise, **do not race the vans.** Take your time, pull over to let faster traffic pass, and use your mirrors. The first 135 km are the most dangerous for tired or overconfident riders.
</Aside>
