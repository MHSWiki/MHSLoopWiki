Heading **Counter-Clockwise** (Chiang Mai -> Pai -> Mae Hong Son -> Mae Sariang) is the choice for those who want to jump straight into the technical essence of the loop.

**Why choose Counter-Clockwise?**
You tackle the most famous (and most technical) section of the road—the 762 curves from Chiang Mai to Pai—on Day 1 while your energy and concentration levels are at their peak. It also means you finish the loop on the faster, smoother Highway 108, which is a relief when you're fatigued on the final day.

## 🗺️ "Have More Time?" — Side Trips & Detours

Even on a counter-clockwise loop, these detours are highly recommended if your schedule allows:

### 1. Ban Rak Thai (Overnight Extension)
*   **From:** Mae Hong Son City.
*   **Action:** Take the steep climb up to the Chinese border village.
*   **Stay:** Stay at **Lee Wine Ruk Thai** for the famous vineyard cabins.

### 2. Soppong / Tham Lod (The Cave Stop)
*   **From:** Between Pai and MHS City.
*   **Action:** Don't just ride past. Visit the massive Tham Lod cave.
*   **Stay:** **Cave Lodge** is the legendary hiker/biker stay in the area.

### 3. The 1263 "Mountain Shortcut" to Mae Chaem
*   **From:** Khun Yuam.
*   **Action:** Instead of going to Mae Sariang, head East on the 1263. It's a rough, remote mountain road that leads to **Mae Chaem**.
*   **Stay:** **Mae Chaem** for a completely authentic, non-tourist Thai experience.

<Aside type="danger" title="Day 1 Warning: Don't Rush to Pai">
Highway 1095 is narrow and has significant minibus traffic. If you're riding counter-clockwise, **do not race the vans.** Take your time, pull over to let faster traffic pass, and enjoy the scenery. The first 135km are the most dangerous for tired or overconfident riders.
</Aside>
