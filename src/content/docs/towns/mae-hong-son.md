Nestled in a deep valley surrounded by mist-shrouded peaks, Mae Hong Son City is the namesake and geographic midpoint of the loop. It is much quieter, more traditional, and more deeply influenced by Shan (Burmese) culture than Pai.

## 🏨 Where to Stay (All Budgets)

The best of Mae Hong Son revolves around the beautiful central lake (Nong Chong Kham).

**Backpacker / Budget**
*   **Crossroads House:** A clean, incredibly friendly, and cheap option favored by budget riders.
*   **PLP Guesthouse:** Basic, reliable rooms with good parking and a welcoming host.

**Mid-Range (GT-Rider Favorites)**
*   **Piya Guesthouse:** The absolute classic rider’s choice. Located right alongside the central lake with a great pool, good breakfast, and secure motorcycle parking.
*   **Baan Phuthadol:** A slightly quieter, modern boutique option just outside the immediate town center.
*   **Boondee House:** Spotlessly clean, affordable teakwood rooms near the morning market.

**Premium / Nature Retreat**
*   **The Imperial Mae Hong Son Resort:** The grand dame of the town. Large rooms with local ethnic décor and expansive grounds.
*   **Fern Resort:** Located a few kilometers out of town in the jungle. A stunning, eco-friendly retreat that famously hosted Angelina Jolie.

## 🍜 Where to Eat
*   **Nong Chong Kham Night Market:** Every evening, food stalls set up around the lake. The best place for cheap, authentic local food while watching the dramatically lit temples reflect in the water.
*   **The Golden Teak Restaurant:** Located inside the Imperial Resort, offering excellent, slightly upscale northern Thai and international cuisine.
*   **Salween River Restaurant:** A long-standing traveler favorite near the lake, serving an eclectic mix of Thai, Burmese, and Western food. Great place for a cold beer after a long ride.

## 📍 Must-See Stops
*   **Wat Chong Kham & Wat Chong Klang:** The iconic, Burmese-style twin temples sitting right on the central lake.
*   **Wat Phra That Doi Kong Mu:** Ride up the incredibly steep hill overlooking the town. The panoramic views of the valley are spectacular, especially at sunrise or sunset.
*   **Su Tong Pae Bridge:** A beautiful bamboo bridge spanning across rice fields, located a short ride north of the city.
