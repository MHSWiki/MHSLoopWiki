# 🌈 Pai: The Town That Stole Your Soul (And Your Brakes)

You’ve just survived the first 762 curves from Chiang Mai. Your hands are probably vibrating and you’ve definitely seen your life flash before your eyes at least once. **Welcome to Pai.** 

Pai is weird. It’s part hippie commune, part party town, and 100% beautiful. Some people stay for a night; some people come for a night and end up staying for three years. For a loop rider, it’s the perfect place to grab a burger, soak in a hot spring, and prep for the next leg of the journey.

---

## 🍺 The One-Beer Summary
*   **The Vibe:** Dreadlocks, yoga pants, and a thousand scooters.
*   **The Stay:** Riverside is peaceful; the center is noisy. Choose wisely.
*   **The Food:** Night Market starts at 6 PM. Go hungry.
*   **Pro Tip:** Park your bike. Walk the town. Your butt needs the break.

## 🍜 The Pai Food Map

If you only eat three things in Pai, make them these:

1.  **Maya Burger Queen:** Listen, I know you came to Thailand for Thai food, but after 762 curves, you need a burger the size of your helmet and a pile of chunky chips. This is an institution.
2.  **Charlie & Lek:** Authentic Northern Thai. Get the **Khao Soi** (Curry Noodles). It’s the soul food of the north.
3.  **The Night Market:** Just walk the main street. Get the bamboo herbal tea, the gyoza, and the "Pai Pancake."

---

## 📍 Don't Miss These (If You Can Still Move)

*   **Pai Canyon:** It’s 10 mins south of town. **Sunset is the only time to go.** Don't wear flip-flops; the ridges are narrow and the drops are real.
*   **Sai Ngam HotSpring:** About 20 mins north. It’s crystal clear, hidden in the jungle, and usually much quieter than the "main" hot springs.
*   **Tham Lod Cave:** It’s a bit of a trek (45 mins towards Soppong), but taking a bamboo raft through a pitch-black cave filled with bats is a vibe you won't forget.

---

<Aside type="caution" title="Expert Advice: The 'Pai Tattoo'">
    You see those people walking around with bandages on their legs? That’s the "Pai Tattoo"—an exhaust burn from a scooter. **Always** get off your bike on the left side, away from the hot pipe. Don't be a statistic.
</Aside>
