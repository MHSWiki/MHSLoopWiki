Located a dizzying 762 curves from Chiang Mai, Pai is a bohemian enclave tucked into a picturesque river valley. It is by far the most heavily touristed stop on the loop, famous for its night market, waterfalls, and relaxed café culture.

## 🏨 Where to Stay (All Budgets)

Pai has accommodation for every type of traveler. Book ahead during the high season (November - February).

**Backpacker / Hostel**
*   **Up2U Guesthouse:** A legendary, budget-friendly spot with a deeply relaxed, communal atmosphere right by the river. Lots of hammocks.
*   **Spicypai Backpackers:** The quintessential party hostel. Great for meeting other solo riders, but don't expect a quiet night.

**Mid-Range (GT-Rider Favorites)**
*   **Prawdao:** Centrally located yet quiet, offering clean, air-conditioned bungalows with off-street parking—a massive plus for motorcyclists.
*   **Pai Chan:** Famous for its pool overlooking the rice paddies and incredibly charming bungalows. Consistently praised in rider communities.

**Premium / Boutique**
*   **Rim Pai Cottages:** Located right on the river in the heart of town. Offers beautiful wooden cottages and lush gardens without sacrificing walking distance to the nightlife.
*   **Reverie Siam:** For ultimate luxury. A stunning, slightly out-of-town resort with distinct colonial architecture, fine dining, and absolute tranquility.

## 🍜 Where to Eat
*   **The Witching Well:** An institution in Pai. Extensive menu, fantastic Italian Bites, curated wines, and a great open-air vibe for watching the town go by.
*   **Charlie & Lek:** A family-run favorite serving some of the best, most authentic Northern Thai dishes (Khao Soi, Green Curry) in town.
*   **Maya Burger Queen:** When you need a break from rice and noodles. Excellent homemade chunky chips, fresh buns, and massive burgers.
*   **Pai Walking Street:** The entire main street transforms into a street food paradise every evening.

## 📍 Must-See Stops
*   **Pai Canyon (Kong Lan):** Best at sunset. Narrow, dust ridges with severe drops—wear proper shoes.
*   **Tham Lod Cave:** About 45 minutes outside of Pai towards Soppong. Massive limestone caves you explore via bamboo rafts.
*   **Bamboo Bridge (Boon Ko Ku So):** A beautiful, peaceful walk suspended over working rice paddies.
