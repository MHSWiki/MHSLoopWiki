Mae Sariang is the quiet, unassuming gem of the loop. Located on the southern arc (Highway 108), it lacks the heavy tourism of Pai, offering a deeply authentic, peaceful Thai provincial experience along the banks of the Yuam River. 

## 🏨 Where to Stay (All Budgets)

Most of the best accommodation in Mae Sariang flanks the river, providing perfect relaxing views after a day of riding.

**Backpacker / Budget**
*   **Mae Sariang Guesthouse:** Very cheap, basic, but clean rooms. A staple for backpackers.
*   **Just A Dream Guesthouse:** Highly rated by budget travelers for its friendly owners and cozy atmosphere.

**Mid-Range (Rider Favorites)**
*   **Riverhouse Hotel (The Teak House):** Not to be confused with the 'Resort'. This is a stunning, massive teakwood building. It’s an atmospheric, historical place to stay.
*   **Good View Guesthouse:** Lives up to its name. Clean, affordable riverside rooms with a great attached restaurant.
*   **Northwest Guesthouse:** A classic, reliable,, no-fuss option frequently used by motorcycle tour groups.

**Premium / Top Pick**
*   **Riverhouse Resort:** The legendary GT-Rider focal point in Mae Sariang. It offers modern, incredibly comfortable rooms with balconies directly overlooking the river, a lovely pool, and fantastic food.

## 🍜 Where to Eat
*   **Above the Sea Bar & Restaurant:** Despite the name, this is a great little spot serving excellent Thai dishes, good breakfasts, and cold beer with a relaxed vibe.
*   **Inthira Restaurant:** A long-standing local favorite located on the main road (not the river). Known for serving consistently excellent, authentic Thai food to locals and travelers alike.
*   **Coriander in Teak:** Attached to the Riverhouse Hotel, offering dining inside a beautiful teak structure with a mix of local and Western cafe options. 

## 📍 Must-See Stops
Mae Sariang is more about *vibe* than major tourist attractions, but there are a few notable stops:
*   **Wat Jong Sung & Wat Sri Bunruang:** Elaborate, beautiful Burmese-style temples in the center of town.
*   **Salawin National Park HQ:** A short ride out of town allows you to see the massive Salawin River, which forms the border with Myanmar. 
*   **The Riverside Sunset:** Grab a drink at any riverside guesthouse and watch the sun dip behind the Burmese mountains. It's the quintessential Mae Sariang experience.
